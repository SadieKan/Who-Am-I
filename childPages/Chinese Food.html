Dumplings (Jiaozi/饺子)

  • Thin layer of dough wrapped around filling. This filling may include vegetables, pork, and mutton.
  • They were created by the physician, Zhang Zhongjing to treat frostbite.
  • It is eaten on many holidays/celebrations, such as Chinese New Year.

Chinese Noodles (Miàn Tiáo/面条)

  • My favorite foods are Chinese noodles, more specifically, _xiao mian_. Similar to how there is a variety of different types of pasta dishes, there is an abundant assortment of different Chinese noodle dishes.
  • Some of my favorite noodle dishes:
    • Chongqing Xiao Mian: Spicy noodle dish served with thin noodles and usually a type of meat. The flavor is _"mala"_, which is spicy and numb.
    • Zhajiangmian: This dish consists of a "fried sauce" containing fermented yellow soybean paste and a bit of sweat fermented wheat paste. In Mandarin, _zhajiang_ means "fried sauce". They also have something similar called jjajangmyeon in Korea.
    • Sichuan Noodles: A spicy cold noodle dish that is also famous for its spicy and numbing flavors.

Tangyuan (Tāngyuán/汤圆)

  • It is a boiled glutinous rice ball with filling and traditionally eaten on the day of every Lantern Festival and Winter Solstice. The filling is usually black sesame. Though now, you can find ones with fillings such as rose, walnut kernel, date paste, etc...

Mooncake (Yuèbĭng/月饼)

  • Mooncake is a Chinese pastry that consists of a thick pastry skin filled with a sweet, sometimes savory, filling that is usually made with lotus paste. They often contain a salted egg yolk in the center that represents the moon. They are often decorated with Chinese characters and symbols along with flowers, vines, and animals It is a tradition to eat mooncakes during the Mid-Autumn Festival.

Zongzi (粽子)

  • The traditional food of the Dragon Boat Festival, zongzi, consists of steamed glutinous rice wrapped in reed leaves often with a filling such as red bean, a red date, rose, and bean paste. It is sometimes eaten with sugar. Zongzi used to be used to worship ancestors and gods.

Steamed Stuffed Buns (Baozi/包子)

  • Steamed stuffed buns are similar to dumplings, except they are larger and have a thicker/more airy outer layer. The stuffing can vary but was originally minced beef and mutton. Though the origins are unclear, it is thought to be created as sacrifices for dead soldiers in battles by Zhuge Liang (politician and military strategist).
