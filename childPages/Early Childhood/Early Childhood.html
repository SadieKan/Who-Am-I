I was born in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. At the time my parents did not have much money as they had only recently moved to Canada a few years before I was born and because they did not want any money from my relatives. About a year after I was born, I was brought to China by my grandma. I stayed in China and was raised by my Grandma for 2 years, while my parents focused on their work in Canada. My parents would visit me as often as they could. Eventually, I came back to Canada with my grandparents. Because I had spent quite a lot of time in China, my first language was Mandarin and I often found it difficult to talk to people in my pre-school. After a few years in Canada, my first language became English. Though I got better at English, I started to forget words and phrases in Chinese. Eventually, my parents enrolled me in Chinese classes. I have stopped taking these Chinese classes now since my Mandarin had gotten better (I still sometimes forget basic words, especially when writing).

Aside from Chinese class, I took many different out of school classes. I had started dance classes when I was 3 and art classes (which I stopped taking) at age 5. Some of the sports that I did growing up include soccer, badminton, swimming, skiing, and volleyball.
